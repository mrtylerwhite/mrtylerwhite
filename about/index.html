Principal Product Designer · Builder Kickoff™

The prototype is not the deliverable. The decision is.

I got into design by accident.

Wanted to do 3D animation for films. Coasted on natural talent. Couldn't find a job. Spent a year unemployed, living at home, applying to nothing because nothing was biting.

Went back to school. Saved a full year to pay for it. Didn't speak to a single classmate for 10 months. Just worked. Stayed late. Didn't want friends. Wanted to be good.

Portfolio day: got picked by the top company in the room. Negotiated $40k when they offered $30k.

First real job: the senior designer quit two months in. No mentor. No protection. Sink or swim.

I stopped copying what the last designer did and started thinking from first principles. Actual research. Actual opinion. Actual bets. That's when the work started landing.

Four and a half years later I was the lead. User base: 1M to 3.2M. Then Director of UX. Team of three. Built a product from zero that hit $1.1M ARR in its first year and grew to 50% of company revenue.

Then got let go. Twice. Same year.

The arrogant version of me didn't survive that. A clearer one did.

For most of my career I obsessed over craft. Perfect dashboards. Pixel-perfect login screens. I didn't know I should be tying any of it to business outcomes. Then I learned about time to value. Everything changed.

That clarity became the Builder Kickoff™. A facilitation framework for turning a business problem into an aligned team decision before anyone opens Figma. Teams walk in with a fuzzy idea. They leave with scope, a business case, and a plan.

16 years in the startup wild west. Principal Product Designer. Based in Montreal.

Outside of that: husband, new dad, early mornings, too much coffee. Less interested in output for its own sake. More interested in work that actually moves something.
